# Art Direction

## Target

Use the saved exploration/parallax mockup as the north star:

`assets/concepts/blood-vessel-exploration-reference.png`

The game should feel like swimming through a living blood vessel, but the implementation should stay friendly to a small 2D browser game.

## Buildable Visual Rules

- Vessel walls are wavy bands with simple gradients and highlighted edges.
- Red blood cells are reusable biconcave oval sprites.
- Viruses are circular sprites with short spikes and strong color silhouettes.
- The white blood cell is a soft blob with a few pseudopod bumps and simple eyes.
- Antibody shots are cyan Y-shaped bolts with small glowing trails.
- Depth comes from parallax, scale differences, and sparse drifting particles.
- Platelet hazards should read as clustered yellow bubbles rather than single rocks.
- Branch openings and wall-cell texture should be used sparingly to echo the concept image without hiding enemies.
- Avoid branchy, tree-like background structures; they read as the wrong material for this game.

## Keep Avoiding

- Cinematic 3D lighting that cannot be reproduced in the game.
- Dense background detail that hides enemies.
- Medical horror or gore.
- Realistic weapons.
- One-note red visuals without cyan, yellow, green, or purple gameplay accents.
